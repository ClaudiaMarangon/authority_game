You will play a simple game called the Task Game, which is a game for two players: an Authority and a Subordinate. As the name suggests, in this game, there is a task to be performed. There is a payment reward for both you and your partner if the task is performed, and either or both of you can perform it. Each player decides independently whether to perform the task or not. However:

  • If neither of you perform the task, there is no reward
  • Performing the task requires effort, so whoever performs it will have to pay a cost.
Specifically:
  • If the Authority performs the task alone and the Subordinate does not perform the task, the Authority receives 5 and the Subordinate receives 10
  • If the Authority does not perform the task and the Subordinate performs the task alone, the Authority receives 10 and the Subordinate receives 7
  • If both players perform the task, the Authority receives 5 and the Subordinate receives 7
  • If both players do not perform the task, the Authority receives 3 and the Subordinate receives 5.

Each player will choose independently whether they want to perform the task or not, and then the computer will match the answers and calculate the appropriate payoffs.

However, before the choice, the Authority can make one more decision. The Authority can promise a bonus payment, to be paid by the Authority to the Subordinate ONLY IF the Subordinate chooses to perform the task. The Authority can offer bonus payments of 2 or 4, or of nothing at all (0).
